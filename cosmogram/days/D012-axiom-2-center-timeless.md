## Topic

Lock Axiom 2: *“Center is timeless. Periphery is temporal.”*

## Outcome — Axiom 2 locked

> The eternal center is not a location within reality but the **timeless substrate from which time emerges**. Periphery is what observers experience as temporal sequence. Time is not a container the universe sits inside; it is a property that arises when observers are entangled with what they observe. The center is what remains when entanglement is removed.
>
> **Anchor:** Wheeler-DeWitt equation (canonical quantum gravity, no global time parameter) + Page-Wootters mechanism (time as observer–system entanglement).
>
> ## Why this framing won
>
> Three options were tested:
>
> - **A. Immutability.** *Failed.* Every candidate either changes or has unknown change-status.
> - - **B. Epistemic hiddenness.** *Failed.* Makes the axiom about us, not the structure.
>   - - **C. Nāgārjuna / Heidegger move.** Center is the **precondition** that makes the change/no-change binary intelligible. *Selected.*
>    
>     - Option C alone risks pure mysticism, so a physics anchor (Wheeler-DeWitt + Page-Wootters) was added — not as proof, but as scaffolding critics cannot dismiss as pure intuition.
>    
>     - ## Side outcomes

> - **Weak Convergence locked as a principle:** inner-access claims (mystical / contemplative) and outer physics need not match in form, only in pointing-direction.
> - - A full private cosmology (souls as droplets of ocean consciousness, three bodies, mind as charioteer, liberation as natural state when anchors are removed) was scored against Indian textual sources: 6 exact matches (Advaita, Katha Upanishad, Yoga Sutras, Bhagavad Gita, vasana doctrine), 2 close, 1 partial divergence. Recorded for reference, not folded into the spine.
